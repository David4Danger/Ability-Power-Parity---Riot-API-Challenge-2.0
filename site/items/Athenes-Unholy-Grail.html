Athene's Unholy Grail had no statistical changes, instead it had a more forgiving build path. That creates a stronger early game where it can compete with the likes of Needlessly Large Rod. As intended, the winrate of Athene's went up by a healthy amount, no longer the lowest winrate of the 10 AP items changed in patch 5.13.

"ARE THESE CHOICES MEANINGFUL? You tell us." The answer was yes, Riot. Even if Athene's is just a cup.

Orianna built Athene's 54.38% of the time in patch 5.11.
Orianna built Athene's 52.14% of the time in patch 5.14.
Orianna built Athene's -2.24% less after is was updated.

While overall Athene's suffered from being built less often overall, Orianna players for the most part stuck with the item. Despite the only change being an easier build path, a 2% loss in popularity could be attributed to Orianna players branching out for different items that were changed in this patch.

Turkey really isn't interested in buying Athene's - both before and after the changes made to it's build path. On patch 5.11, Turkey had an average 0.15 Athene's built per game, compared to the global average of 0.28 Athene's per game. On patch 5.14, Turkey had an average 0.13 Athene's built per game, compared to the global average of 0.25 Athene's per game.

Turkey as a whole is going to go OOM if they don't get on that Athene's game!

On patch 5.11, only one item managed a sub-50% win ratio in any given region: Athene's in NA at 49.50%, and Athene's in OCE at 49.70%. Other than those two, no other item in any region managed to be lower than 50%.

On patch 5.14, Athene's in both NA and OCE managed to have a >50% win ratio. After this increase, no AP item managed to have less than a 50% win ratio. Athene's and Urgot both have a winrate above 50%, what a time to be alive.
